Letter Home
Oil on linen · 100 × 75 cm · 2024
Signed and dated verso · Unique work, one of a kind
Represented by Blonde Contemporary, London

Letter Home was painted in the second winter of Wilde's interior light series, in her father's flat after his death. The work folds Hampstead's grey winter daylight into a single still life: a folded letter on a wooden ledge, almost out of frame. Where Threshold treats light as architecture, Letter Home treats it as memory. The painting was acquired by a Dubai-based collector during the work's first preview week.

Sophia Wilde (b. 1989, London) makes paintings of interior light. After studying at the Slade and a residency in Lisbon, she has shown at Blonde Contemporary, London (2022, 2024) and in group shows at Tabula Rasa, Hauser & Wirth Editions, and the Approach. Her work is held in private collections in the UK, UAE, and Germany.

Wilde will release her first signed print edition with EchoFour in July 2026 (Vault, edition of 250).

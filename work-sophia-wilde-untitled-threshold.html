Untitled (Threshold) belongs to Wilde's 2025 series of interior light studies. Painted from observation in her father's flat in Hampstead during the long winter, the work treats domestic threshold spaces — door frames, window ledges — as sites where light becomes a kind of architecture. Wilde works slowly, often returning to the same room for weeks. The result is a painting that holds time.

Sophia Wilde (b. 1989, London) makes paintings of interior light. After studying at the Slade and a residency in Lisbon, she has shown at Blonde Contemporary, London (2022, 2024) and in group shows at Tabula Rasa, Hauser & Wirth Editions, and the Approach. Her work is held in private collections in the UK, UAE, and Germany.
